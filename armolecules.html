Challenge

🧬 Protein molecules are inherently complex and challenging to visualize in three dimensions.

📚 Traditional textbook images and static diagrams often fail to convey the true structure and dynamics of these molecules, making them difficult for students to grasp.

🎓 Students at Wake Tech University needed an interactive tool to help them better understand and explore molecular structures.

Solution

🌟 We leveraged advancements in augmented reality to develop an immersive application, enabling students to visualize molecules in their own environment.

🖐️ The app allows users to rotate, scale, and interact with molecular models using intuitive touch gestures, while answering embedded questions to reinforce learning.

📈 This approach led to improved student comprehension and provided instructors with better tools for assessment.

My Role

💡 Led the redesign of the frontend, empowering instructors to upload and configure molecules for student exploration.

📷 Implemented camera functionality for QR code recognition, enabling students to access specific molecular models seamlessly.

🗄️ Developed backend CRUD operations with Spring Boot and Hibernate to manage molecules and assignments efficiently.
